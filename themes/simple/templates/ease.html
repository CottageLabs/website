Reduce cognitive load in data-rich repositories with EASE

A practical checklist for data repositories

This checklist is a practical design and review aid. It contains a set of questions to keep in mind while designing or reviewing a page, feature, or workflow.

It is not a full accessibility audit.

For more detail, see the COGA guidance and the GOV.UK cognitive accessibility guidelines in the resources below.

ExplainHelp users understand what things are and how to use them.
ArrangeHelp users find what they need and follow a clear structure.
SimplifyUse clear content and remove unnecessary complexity.
EnableSupport users with errors, memory, help, and personalisation.

How to use this checklist

Use it as a quality standard: ask these questions during design, and ask them again when reviewing the page. Tick the checkbox next to each statement if it is true for your website or service. If you are not sure, treat it as something to investigate.

Done is better than perfect: even a few improvements can make a real difference to users.

COGA guideline areas

  1. Help users understand what things are and how to use them.
  2. Help users find what they need.
  3. Use clear and understandable content.
  4. Help users avoid mistakes and know how to correct them.
  5. Help users focus.
  6. Ensure processes do not rely on memory.
  7. Provide help and support.
  8. Support adaptation and personalisation.
